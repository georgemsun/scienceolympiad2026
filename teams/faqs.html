Frequently Asked Questions

This page contains answers to frequently asked questions specific to the 2026 Science Olympiad National Tournament. Please note that these FAQs are different from the event-specific Frequently Asked Questions linked on the Resources page.

Have a question that you need answered? Contact us.

Events

Are teams allowed to practice in the event venues before the competition begins on Saturday?

No, General Rule #5 prohibits this as tournament organizers have not given permission to do so.

Can teams bring more than 6 alternates to compete in the trial events?

No, there is a maximum of 6 alternates.

What events can parents and supporters watch?

Guests are welcome to view the following events:

Division B — Boomilever, Helicopter, Hovercraft, Mission Possible, Ping Pong Parachute, Scrambler

Division C — Boomilever, Electric Vehicle, Helicopter, Hovercraft, Robot Tour

If I have an event conflict, may I attend an event at a block outside of the block corresponding to my team number?

No, all competitors must attend events during the blocks corresponding to their team numbers on the tournament schedule. No swaps or changes are permitted. Students arriving late may be allowed to compete at the Event Supervisorâ€™s discretion, but they will not be given additional time.

Accommodations & Transportation

Where can teams stay for the duration of the competition?

Teams may choose to stay on campus in USC dormitory housing, which includes a meal package, or arrange their own lodging and meals off campus. More information about on-campus housing and meal packages will be provided in the coming months. For off-campus options, visit the Travel & Accommodations and Places to Eat pages.

Ceremonies & Awards

What can I bring to the Swap Meet?

All students must follow the rules for the Swap Meet, which are posted on the Ceremonies page.

Is there a dress code for the Awards & Closing Ceremonies?

Yes, there is a dress code for the Awards & Closing Ceremony. Please see the Ceremonies page for details.

How many medals and trophies will be awarded?

Medals will be awarded to the top six finishers in each event for each division. Trophies will be awarded to the top ten teams in each division.
